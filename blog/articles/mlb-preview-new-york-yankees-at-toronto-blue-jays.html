MLB Preview: New York Yankees at Toronto Blue Jays

Published: June 12, 2026

The Toronto Blue Jays open a weekend series against the New York Yankees on Friday, June 12, 2026, at Rogers Centre. First pitch is scheduled for 7:07 p.m. ET under clear skies with the roof likely open and a light wind blowing in from left field.

The Lines

Sports Select has priced this as a near coin-flip:

  • Moneyline: Blue Jays +135 (2.35) | Yankees +145 (2.45) | Tie +190 (2.90)
  • Run line: Blue Jays -0.5 at 1.76 | Yankees +0.5 at 1.80
  • Total: Over 8 at 1.85 | Under 8 at 1.75

Toronto is a slight favorite on the run line, reflecting the home-field advantage and a favorable pitching matchup.

Matchup Angles

The Blue Jays are expected to face left-hander Ryan Weathers, who has allowed 1.67 home runs per nine innings this season. That plays directly into Toronto's recent power surge. Over the last five games, Ernie Clement has driven in 19 runs with two home runs and a scalding 1.316 OPS, while George Springer and Alejandro Kirk have also produced hard contact. Vladimir Guerrero Jr. has yet to homer in this five-game stretch, but his .111 OPS is an outlier that should regress against a homer-prone lefty.

The Yankees counter with a deep lineup, but Rogers Centre's dimensions and the warm 79°F evening should favor hitters on both sides. The total sits at 8 with the under juiced to 1.75, suggesting the market expects a lower-scoring game than the matchup data implies.

The Pick

The value lies with the Blue Jays on the run line at -0.5 (1.76). Weathers' HR/9 rate and Toronto's current form at home create a clear edge. If you prefer a safer moneyline play, the Blue Jays at +135 is also reasonable for a team that has won two of its last three.

Bottom Line

Toronto has the park, the matchup, and the momentum. Look for the Blue Jays to take the series opener against the Yankees.
