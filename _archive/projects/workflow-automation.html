Workflow Automation

Replacing manual processes with automated systems across healthcare, construction, retail, and real estate.

Back to Timeline

The Problem

Too many businesses run on email chains, paper forms, and Excel spreadsheets passed between departments. It's slow, error-prone, and impossible to audit. I've seen healthcare clinics lose patient referrals in inboxes, construction firms delay projects because approval signatures were on vacation, and retail stores reconcile inventory by hand every Sunday night.

The Solution

I design and build electronic workflow systems that:

  • Replace paper forms with digital submissions and validation
  • Route requests automatically to the right approvers based on rules
  • Send notifications, reminders, and escalations without human intervention
  • Log every action for full audit trails and compliance reporting
  • Integrate with existing systems (SharePoint, CRMs, accounting software)

Industries Served

Healthcare

Patient intake, referral tracking, equipment requests, and staff scheduling workflows.

Construction

Change order approvals, safety incident reports, subcontractor onboarding, and RFIs.

Retail

Inventory replenishment, return authorizations, and district manager approvals.

Real Estate

Lease approvals, maintenance requests, tenant screening, and vendor payments.

Technology

Microsoft Power Automate SharePoint Workflows PowerApps Python / Flask REST APIs SQL Server Email Integration

What I Learned

The hardest part of workflow automation isn't the technology — it's the politics. Getting departments to agree on who approves what, mapping processes that have never been formally documented, and convincing people that "the way we've always done it" isn't sacred. The technical build is the easy part. Change management is the real deliverable.
