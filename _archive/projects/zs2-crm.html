ZS2 Technologies CRM Implementation

Enterprise-wide CRM deployment and third-party application integration for a growing construction-tech company.

Back to Timeline

The Context

ZS2 Technologies is a construction-tech company building advanced building materials and infrastructure solutions. When I joined as IT Manager in April 2022, the company was scaling fast — but customer data was scattered across spreadsheets, email threads, and individual workstations. Sales, project management, and support teams were working in silos, and leadership had no unified view of the customer lifecycle.

The Project

I led the evaluation, selection, and implementation of an enterprise CRM platform tailored to construction-industry workflows. This wasn't just a software install — it was a business transformation.

  • Requirements Gathering: Interviewed sales, project management, finance, and executive teams to map current processes and pain points
  • Platform Selection: Evaluated Salesforce, HubSpot, and Zoho against ZS2's integration needs, budget, and growth trajectory
  • Data Migration: Cleansed and migrated 5+ years of customer, vendor, and project data from spreadsheets and legacy systems
  • Third-Party Integration: Connected the CRM to accounting software, project management tools, and the company website lead forms
  • Workflow Automation: Built automated lead routing, follow-up reminders, and pipeline stage triggers
  • Training & Rollout: Delivered hands-on training sessions and created internal documentation for ongoing support

Outcomes

Unified View

Leadership gained real-time visibility into sales pipeline, customer interactions, and project status.

Faster Response

Automated lead routing reduced response time from days to hours.

Data Integrity

Eliminated duplicate records and established a single source of truth for customer data.

What I Learned

CRM implementations fail when they're treated as IT projects instead of business projects. Success required me to become a temporary product manager — translating business needs into technical requirements, managing vendor relationships, and coaching staff through change. The technology was the easy part. Getting people to trust and use the system was the real challenge.
