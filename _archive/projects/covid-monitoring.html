COVID-19 Patient Monitoring Deployment

Emergency infrastructure deployment for real-time patient tracking across Saskatchewan Health Authority facilities during the pandemic.

Back to Timeline

The Crisis

In early 2020, the Saskatchewan Health Authority faced an unprecedented challenge: tracking COVID-19 patients across dozens of hospitals, long-term care facilities, and testing sites — all while infection rates climbed and staff were stretched to breaking point. Existing systems weren't designed for real-time pandemic surveillance at provincial scale.

The Deployment

I led the technical deployment and implementation of patient monitoring infrastructure under extreme urgency and tight deadlines:

  • Infrastructure Scaling: Rapidly provisioned additional server capacity and network bandwidth to handle surging data volumes
  • Monitoring Platform: Deployed and configured real-time patient tracking systems across multiple facilities
  • Integration: Connected monitoring data to existing health information systems and reporting dashboards
  • Security Hardening: Ensured patient data remained protected under heightened threat conditions
  • 24/7 Support: Provided round-the-clock escalation support during peak waves to ensure zero downtime
  • Staff Training: Trained clinical and IT staff on new systems under pressure, with minimal disruption to patient care

The Stakes

This wasn't a typical IT project. Every hour of delay meant slower contact tracing, delayed resource allocation, and increased risk to vulnerable populations. The infrastructure I deployed had to work perfectly from day one — there was no room for a "soft launch."

Windows Server SQL Server PowerBI Network Security Disaster Recovery Health Information Systems

What I Learned

Emergency IT teaches you what really matters. Documentation, change control, and careful planning are luxuries you don't always have. What you need is decisiveness, clear communication, and the ability to improvise without breaking things. I learned to trust my instincts, delegate under pressure, and accept that "good enough today" beats "perfect next week" when lives are on the line.
