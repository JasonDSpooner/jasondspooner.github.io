SHA PowerBI Systems & Reporting

Enterprise business intelligence administration enabling data-driven decisions across Saskatchewan's province-wide health organization.

Back to Timeline

The Challenge

The Saskatchewan Health Authority serves millions of residents across hundreds of facilities — hospitals, clinics, long-term care homes, and mental health centers. Decisions about staffing, equipment, budgets, and patient care need accurate, timely data. But that data was locked in silos: financial systems, patient databases, HR platforms, and operational spreadsheets.

My Role

As Senior Analyst, I administered the enterprise PowerBI environment that became the single pane of glass for health authority leadership:

  • Environment Management: Maintained PowerBI Premium capacity, gateway configurations, and data refresh schedules
  • Data Model Support: Supported complex data models connecting SQL Server, SharePoint lists, and external health data sources
  • Report Development: Built and maintained executive dashboards for patient volumes, wait times, staffing levels, and financial performance
  • Performance Tuning: Optimized slow-running reports by refactoring DAX measures, improving query folding, and resizing datasets
  • Security & Compliance: Configured row-level security to ensure users only saw data for their region, facility, or department
  • User Support: Trained departmental analysts and troubleshooted report issues across the organization

Impact

Real-Time Visibility

Leadership moved from monthly static reports to daily interactive dashboards.

Self-Service Analytics

Departmental analysts could build their own reports without waiting for central IT.

Data Trust

Standardized metrics and definitions reduced conflicting numbers across departments.

Technology

PowerBI Premium DAX Power Query (M) SQL Server SharePoint Lists On-Premises Data Gateway Row-Level Security

What I Learned

Data is only valuable if people trust it. I spent as much time building relationships with department heads and validating numbers as I did writing DAX. The technical skills got the dashboards built — but the political skills got them used. I also learned that performance optimization in PowerBI is an art: sometimes the fix is a better data model, sometimes it's just removing a visual nobody looks at.
