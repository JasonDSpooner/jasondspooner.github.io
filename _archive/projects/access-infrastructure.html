Access Communications B2B & WiFi Solutions

Selling business cable and internet solutions while building a commercial WiFi product for hotels and hospitality.

Back to Timeline

The Role

Access Communications was a Saskatchewan telecommunications and cable company serving residential and business customers across the province. I joined as Senior Helpdesk Team Leader, but the role blended technical support, B2B sales, and product development in ways I didn't expect.

B2B Cable & Internet Sales

I sold business-grade cable and internet solutions to enterprise clients across Saskatchewan — from small offices to multi-location businesses needing dedicated connectivity:

  • Solution Selling: Met with business owners and IT managers to understand connectivity needs and propose tailored packages
  • Relationship Building: Managed ongoing accounts, ensuring renewals, upgrades, and satisfaction
  • Technical Consultation: Explained bandwidth, SLAs, and infrastructure requirements to non-technical decision makers
  • Cross-Selling: Identified opportunities to add digital TV, phone, and emerging data services to existing accounts

Commercial WiFi Tool

One of the most impactful things I built at Access was a commercial WiFi deployment tool designed for hotels and hospitality venues:

  • The Problem: Hotels needed reliable guest WiFi, but consumer routers couldn't handle hundreds of simultaneous connections across multiple floors
  • The Solution: I developed a configuration and deployment toolkit for commercial-grade wireless access points that could be rolled out across hotel properties
  • Features: Captive portal for guest login, bandwidth management per room, separate staff and guest networks, and centralized monitoring
  • Deployment: The tool was used to deploy WiFi in multiple hotels across Saskatchewan, improving guest satisfaction and creating a new revenue stream for Access

Team Leadership

Beyond sales and product work, I led the technical support team:

  • Managed client relationships and service delivery, consistently meeting or exceeding SLA targets
  • Developed training programs and technical documentation to onboard new staff and reduce repeat tickets
  • Coordinated technology deployments and upgrades across regional offices

What I Learned

This role taught me that the best technical solutions come from understanding real business pain. The WiFi tool wasn't born in a lab — it came from hotel managers complaining about guest complaints. I learned to listen to customers, translate their frustration into requirements, and build something that solved the problem while creating value for the business. It was my first real taste of product development, and it stuck with me.
