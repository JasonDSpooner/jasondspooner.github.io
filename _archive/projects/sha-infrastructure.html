SHA Enterprise Infrastructure & Security

Managing province-wide server infrastructure, security protocols, and high-availability systems for Saskatchewan Health Authority.

Back to Timeline

The Environment

The Saskatchewan Health Authority operates one of the largest IT footprints in the province — hundreds of Windows servers, thousands of workstations, and critical systems that literally keep people alive. When a server goes down, it's not just an inconvenience. It's a potential risk to patient care.

My Responsibilities

Over two decades, I managed the core infrastructure that kept this massive organization running:

  • Server Infrastructure: Administered enterprise Windows server environments including Active Directory, DNS, DHCP, and file/print services
  • Security Protocols: Implemented and maintained network security policies, firewall rules, patch management, and vulnerability remediation
  • High Availability: Designed and maintained clustered server environments with failover capabilities for critical health applications
  • Disaster Recovery: Developed, tested, and maintained DR procedures — including off-site replication and documented recovery runbooks
  • Escalation Support: Served as the final escalation point for complex technical issues that front-line support couldn't resolve
  • Capacity Planning: Monitored resource utilization and forecasted growth to prevent performance bottlenecks before they impacted care

What I Learned

Enterprise infrastructure is invisible work — when it's perfect, nobody notices. The reward is knowing that a nurse can pull up a patient record at 3 AM without thinking about the servers, networks, and security layers that make it possible. I learned that reliability is a culture, not a technology. It's about documentation, testing, paranoia, and humility.
