SharePoint Legacy Support & Development

Maintaining, modernizing, and extending legacy SharePoint environments to keep business workflows running smoothly.

Back to Timeline

The Reality

SharePoint is the system everyone loves to complain about and nobody can replace. For over two decades, organizations have built critical business processes on SharePoint lists, libraries, InfoPath forms, and custom workflows. Tearing it all out isn't realistic — but letting it rot isn't either.

What I Do

  • Farm Health: Monitor, patch, and maintain on-premise SharePoint farms (2010, 2013, 2016, 2019, SE)
  • Workflow Repair: Fix broken SharePoint Designer and InfoPath workflows without rebuilding from scratch
  • Custom Development: Build web parts, event receivers, and PowerShell automation scripts
  • Migration Planning: Assess what can move to SharePoint Online vs. what must stay on-premise
  • Performance Tuning: Resolve search indexing issues, database bloat, and slow page loads

Environments Supported

SharePoint 2010 / 2013 SharePoint 2016 / 2019 / SE SharePoint Online (Hybrid) InfoPath Forms SharePoint Designer Workflows PowerShell Automation SSRS Integration

What I Learned

SharePoint is a Rorschach test — every organization uses it differently, and what works in one farm will break another. The key to supporting legacy SharePoint is patience, documentation, and incremental change. Big bang migrations fail. Small, tested improvements succeed.
