Virtualization Services

Migrating legacy applications and systems to virtualized environments using VMware, Azure, Proxmox, and serverless architectures.

Back to Timeline

The Challenge

Legacy applications running on aging physical servers are a ticking time bomb. Hardware fails, operating systems go end-of-life, and finding parts for a 2012 Dell PowerEdge gets harder every year. But these apps often run critical business processes — payroll, inventory, compliance reporting — and "just rewrite it" isn't in the budget.

The Approach

I migrate legacy systems to virtualized environments that extend their lifespan, improve reliability, and make future moves easier:

  • P2V Migration: Physical-to-virtual conversion of existing servers with minimal downtime
  • Platform Modernization: Moving from outdated hypervisors to current VMware or Proxmox clusters
  • Containerization: Where possible, wrapping legacy apps in Docker containers for portability
  • Serverless Refactoring: Breaking monolithic batch jobs into Azure Functions or Lambda where appropriate
  • Disaster Recovery: Implementing VM-level snapshots and replication for fast recovery

What I Learned

Virtualization isn't just about saving hardware costs — it's about agility. Once a system is virtual, you can snapshot it before an upgrade, replicate it for testing, and move it to new hardware without reinstalling. The peace of mind that comes from knowing you can roll back in 10 minutes is worth the migration effort alone.
