The Zero-Dollar Implementation Plan

Here is exactly how to build everything the proposal promises — website, payments, hosting, inventory tracking — without paying a single platform fee. You only pay when you make a sale.

Step 1: Get Your Code House in Order (GitHub — Free)

  • Create a free GitHub account.
  • Start a new repository named retroresellerssk (or whatever you want to call your store).
  • This is where your website code lives. Every change is tracked. If you break something, you can roll it back instantly.
  • If you ever hire a developer, you just add them to the repo. No file transfers, no lost work.

Step 2: Build the Storefront (Next.js or Astro — Free)

  • Use a free starter template. Search "Next.js e-commerce starter" or "Astro storefront template" on GitHub.
  • These are pre-built websites designed for selling products. You swap in your photos, descriptions, and prices.
  • They come with shopping carts, product galleries, and mobile-friendly design already built.
  • You do not need to code from scratch. You configure.

Step 3: Host It for Free (Vercel — Free Tier)

  • Sign up for free at Vercel.com using your GitHub account.
  • Connect your GitHub repo. Vercel automatically builds and deploys your site every time you push an update.
  • You get a free custom domain option (retroresellerssk.vercel.app) or you can buy a cheap domain (about $12/year) and point it there.
  • SSL certificate, global CDN, and fast loading are all included. No server management. No hosting bills.

Step 4: Take Payments (Stripe — Pay-Per-Sale)

  • Create a free Stripe account.
  • There is no monthly fee. Stripe takes a small percentage of each transaction only when you actually make a sale.
  • Connect Stripe to your storefront template (usually one line of configuration).
  • Customers pay by credit card, Apple Pay, Google Pay — all handled securely by Stripe. You never touch card numbers.
  • Money deposits to your bank account automatically on a schedule you choose.

Step 5: Add Your Products

  • Most starter templates let you manage products by editing simple text files (Markdown or JSON) in your GitHub repo.
  • Add a photo, title, price, and description for each item. Push the change. Your site updates in under a minute.
  • No database to manage. No complex backend. Just files.

Step 6: Connect Your Sales Channels

  • Your new website becomes the central hub. Link to it from Facebook Shop, Instagram, Whatnot, eBay — everywhere you already sell.
  • Use the website for direct sales (you keep 100% minus the Stripe fee) while keeping marketplace listings for discovery.
  • Add a simple newsletter signup (free with Buttondown or Mailchimp free tier) to build a customer list you actually own.
