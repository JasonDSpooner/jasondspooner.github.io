The Therapeutic Power of Coloring

Research Thesis · 2020 – Present

An in-depth thesis examining the scientific evidence behind coloring as a legitimate wellness practice — from its historical roots in art therapy to modern applications in clinical and everyday settings.

The research covers:

  • Mental health benefits — anxiety reduction, stress relief, and meditative states
  • Cognitive development — focus, flow states, and motor skills
  • Creative engagement — benefits across all age groups and abilities
  • Historical context — from early art therapy pioneers to contemporary wellness movements

This work bridges the gap between creative expression and evidence-based wellness, making the case for coloring as a serious tool for mental health and personal development.
